Attachment and Love: Ontological Divergence

Bhakti and Jnana: Parallel Epistemologies of the Absolute

  • Questions all perceptual and conceptual dualities: Who am I?

Though seemingly antithetical, Bhakti begins in duality and dissolves it in longing; Jnana begins with doubt and resolves it in certainty. Each requires the other to fully actualise its telos; modern practitioners often traverse both terrains simultaneously.

Dialectics of Convergence: The Flame and the Mirror

In the advanced stages of inner evolution, the binary between devotion and knowledge begins to dissolve. The fervour of Bhakti matures into luminous clarity; the austerity of Jnana softens into blissful union. The fire (Bhakti) purifies the intellect, while the mirror (Jnana) reflects the flame.
