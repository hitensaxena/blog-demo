Knowledge & Un‑learning: The Paradox of Knowing

Published on June 22, 2025 · Reflections by Hiten

Discursive intellection affords ever finer apprehensions of the world, yet those same operations must ultimately be bracketed if one is to encounter reality in its pre‑conceptual fullness. This essay explores the dialectic between epistemic expansion and apophatic un‑knowing across metaphysics, cognitive science and contemplative praxis【609473967727599†L22-L43】. As labels dissolve, meaning is re‑enchanted; lógos and silence cancel and illuminate each other in equal measure.

Epistemic Proliferation & Dissolution of Taxonomies

Across the sciences, formerly stable ontological kinds — atoms, minds, even matter itself — are dissolving into relational fields【609473967727599†L22-L43】. Quantum field theory supplants classical atomism with probabilities, while post‑structural linguistics interprets language as a matrix of signifying practices rather than a mirror of essences. Vedāntic neti neti (“not this, not that”) echoes the same trajectory: the analytic negation of mis‑identifications culminates not in nihilism but in the disclosure of the unobjectifiable ground of all relations. Knowledge pursued to its logical telos thus abolishes the very labels it refines, reinstalling mystery on a higher register【609473967727599†L22-L43】.

Methodological Abstention & Re‑Enchantment

Conversely, contemplative traditions cultivate strategic epistemic minimisation. Neuroscience correlates such practice with decreased default‑mode network activity and increased global coherence, experientially re‑sacralising immediacy. Apophatic praxis is not anti‑intellectual: only a mind honed by dialectical rigour can suspend itself without regressing into credulous myth. Un‑knowing, therefore, is a second‑order innocence — a mature emptiness distinct from naïveté【609473967727599†L44-L61】.

Phenomenologically, love is an intensified, non‑judicative attention that both assimilates complexity and saturates emptiness with valence. In Sufi hermeneutics, Ibn ʿArabī fuses gnosis with fervor to yield tawḥīd (unitary vision). Manana follows: a critical elaboration and dialectical interrogation of the assimilated corpus. Nididhyāsana is sustained contemplative absorption wherein discursive constructs release themselves. Finally, Līlā — post‑conceptual play — marks re‑entry into the world, wielding concepts lightly as pragmatic instruments. Like indigenous epistemologies, one learns the rivers’ names before discerning their songs beneath language【609473967727599†L78-L90】.

Lógos is wielded as a transparent medium rather than an opaque mirror; discourse persists as a luminous hush within the silence that exceeds it.
